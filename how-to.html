How to

betsteem is a betting exchange powered by the steem blockchain, so the first thing you need to now is how does a betting exchange works:

What is a betting exchange?

A betting exchange is a platform that allow gamblers to bet against each other instead of betting against a bookmaker.

How does a betting exchange works?

Essentially a betting exchange allows any user to take the place of the bookmaker, offering other users the oportunity to place a bet. This is done through a lay bet. By placing a lay bet you allow other users to place a back bet.

Lay bets vs Back bets.

A lay bet means to bet on an outcome to not come true.

Example: bet on "Team A" not to win, this means to lose or draw the match. For competitions where a draw is not possible, like horse raising, a lay bet simply means to bet a horse not to win.

A back bet means to bet on an outcome to come true.

Example: bet on "Team A" to win the match.

On the right side you can see three columns (1, X and 2), each of them containing two sub columns: blue and pink.

The first column (1) targets the first team or player, in this case Portugal, and the subcolumns are for betting that team to win (blue column) or not to win (pink column), namely to back or lay Portugal.

The second column (X) targets a draw in the match, the blue subcolumn used for betting the match will end in a draw and the pink subcolumn used for betting the match will not end in a draw, namely to back or lay the draw.

The third column (2) targets the second team or player, in this case Morocco, and the subcolumns are for betting that team to win (blue column) or not to win (pink column), namely to back or lay Morocco.

The Matched column shows the total ammount of matched bets i.e back bets that have corresponding lay bets.

Each back and lay column will display two values: The first one (in bold) represents the current odds and the second one represents the liquidity.

Liquidity: The liquidity represents the available ammount to bet, this is the total ammount of bets that haven't been matched. So for example in the column 1 you can see the following:

Under the lay box you can see there's a total ammount of $300 waiting to be matched at odds of 1.68, this means that from the total ammount of back bets placed, there are $300 that needs to be matched as lay bets (so the lay box shows $300 on liquidity), so if for example you decide to place a $10 lay bet you've effectively matched $10 of those $300. Now the ammount under the lay box should show $290.

If on the contrary you decide to place a $10 back bet then your bet wouldn't be instantly matched, you'll need to wait for other users to place $300 on lay bets to match those bets placed before yours, then the following $10 will match your back bet. Now the ammount on the lay box would be $310

Placing a lay bet is a little different, in this case the 10 in the stake field means we're giving other users the oportunity to place a bet of $10 on Portugal to win. Contrary to a back bet we're now risking $6.80 (liability) instead of $10 so if Portugal wins we need to pay $6.80 to a user who backed Portugal for $10. If Portugal draw or loses we take the $10 stake from the user who backed Portugal plus our liablity of $6.80 for a total payout of $16.80

Important note:

In betsteem you wont use fiat money to bet, instead you place bets in the form of votes using your Steem Voting Power, we then make the necessary calculations to show you the aproximate USD value.

The USD values showed on the page are aproximate because of the changes in SBD price. On the backend we record all transactions on rshares which is the base value used by Steem to calculate the value of an upvote.
